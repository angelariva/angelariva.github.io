About Me

I'm a PhD student in Applied Mathematics at the Inria Quantic team in Paris, France. Before that I studied in Milan, Italy. My primary research area is Open Quantum Systems theory, with both physical and technological applications in mind. I work with both analytical methods (like time convolutionless master equations and adiabatic elimination) and numerical methods (such as tensor networks for Spin-Boson models, efficient simulation of Lindblad equations, and SDPs). I'm also interested in Many Body theory, especially tensor network simulations of fermionic systems.
